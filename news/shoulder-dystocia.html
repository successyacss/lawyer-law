Overview of birth injuries and their causes

Shoulder dystocia is a rare but serious complication that can occur during childbirth. It occurs when the baby's shoulder gets stuck behind the mother's pubic bone, making it difficult for the baby to be born. There are several potential causes of shoulder dystocia, including:




  1. Fetal macrosomia: This is one of the most common causes of shoulder dystocia. When a baby is larger than average (weighing over 8 pounds, 13 ounces), they are more likely to get stuck during delivery.




  2. Maternal diabetes: Women with diabetes are at an increased risk of having a larger baby, which can lead to shoulder dystocia.




  3. Prolonged labor: If labor is prolonged, the baby may become tired and have difficulty moving through the birth canal, increasing the risk of shoulder dystocia.




  4. Pelvic shape and size: The shape and size of the mother's pelvis can also play a role in causing shoulder dystocia. A narrow or oddly shaped pelvis can make it more likely for the baby's shoulder to get stuck.




  5. Previous history of shoulder dystocia: Women who have had a previous delivery complicated by shoulder dystocia are at an increased risk of experiencing it again in future pregnancies.




It is important for healthcare providers to be aware of these potential causes and take steps to prevent and manage shoulder dystocia during childbirth. By closely monitoring both mother and baby during labor and delivery, healthcare providers can help reduce the risk of this complication and ensure a safe outcome for both mother and baby.
