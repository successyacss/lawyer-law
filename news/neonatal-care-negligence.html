Overview of birth injuries and their causes

Neonatal care negligence is a term used to describe situations where healthcare providers fail to provide proper care for newborn babies. This could include failing to diagnose and treat medical conditions, not monitoring the baby's vital signs closely enough, or making errors during delivery that result in harm to the baby.


Negligence in neonatal care can have serious consequences for both the baby and their family. In some cases, it can lead to long-term health issues or even death. It can also cause emotional distress for parents who trusted their healthcare provider to keep their newborn safe and healthy.


Healthcare providers have a duty of care to their patients, especially vulnerable newborns who are unable to advocate for themselves. When this duty is breached through negligence, it is important for families to seek justice and hold those responsible accountable.


If you believe your child has been a victim of neonatal care negligence, it is important to speak with a qualified attorney who specializes in medical malpractice cases. They can help you understand your legal rights and options for seeking compensation for any harm caused by negligence in neonatal care.
