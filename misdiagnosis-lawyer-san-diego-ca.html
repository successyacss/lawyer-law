Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as medical misdiagnosis?
Medical misdiagnosis occurs when a healthcare provider fails to diagnose a condition accurately or promptly, leading to incorrect or delayed treatment.
How can I prove that my injury was due to a misdiagnosis?
You will need to show that the healthcare provider failed to meet the standard of care expected, and this failure directly caused your harm. This typically requires expert testimony from medical professionals.
What is the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice claim in California?
In California, you generally have one year from the date you discovered (or should have discovered) the injury, but no more than three years from the date of the injury itself.
What types of compensation can I receive if my misdiagnosis claim is successful?
Compensation may include medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, and sometimes punitive damages if egregious negligence is proven.
How much does it cost to hire a medical malpractice lawyer in San Diego?
Many medical malpractice lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case. The fee is usually a percentage of the settlement or award.
