Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as medical malpractice by a doctor?
Medical malpractice occurs when a doctor fails to provide the standard of care that a reasonably competent doctor would, resulting in harm to the patient.
How do I prove medical malpractice occurred?
To prove medical malpractice, you need to show that there was a duty of care, the duty was breached, the breach caused injury, and damages resulted from the injury.
What is the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice claim in Los Angeles?
In California, you generally have three years from the date of injury or one year from discovering the injury to file a medical malpractice lawsuit, whichever comes first.
What compensation can I receive if my case is successful?
You may be entitled to economic damages (medical bills, lost wages) and non-economic damages (pain and suffering) if your case is successful.
Do I need to pay upfront fees for hiring a medical malpractice lawyer?
Many medical malpractice lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case.
