Wrongful death claims

Overview of Gresham medical malpractice laws

Wrongful death is a legal term that refers to a situation where a person dies due to the negligence or misconduct of another individual, company, or entity. In such cases, the surviving family members of the deceased may have grounds to file a wrongful death claim in order to seek compensation for their loss.


Understanding the legal definition of wrongful death is crucial in these situations, as it can help determine whether or not a claim is valid and what course of action should be taken. Generally speaking, in order for a death to be considered wrongful, it must have been caused by someone else's negligence, recklessness, or intentional wrongdoing.


Examples of wrongful death include car accidents caused by drunk drivers, medical malpractice resulting in a patient's death, or accidents on unsafe premises. In these cases, the responsible party can be held liable for damages such as funeral expenses, lost income potential of the deceased, and emotional distress suffered by the surviving family members.


It's important to note that each state has its own laws regarding wrongful death claims, so it's essential to consult with a qualified attorney who specializes in this area of law. They can provide guidance on how to proceed with a claim and ensure that your rights are protected throughout the legal process.


Overall, understanding the legal definition of wrongful death is essential for those who have lost a loved one due to someone else's negligence. By seeking justice through a wrongful death claim, surviving family members can hold responsible parties accountable and receive compensation for their loss.

In a wrongful death claim, there are various types of damages that can be recovered to help ease some of the financial strain on the family. These damages can include compensation for medical expenses incurred before the individual's passing, funeral and burial costs, loss of income and financial support, as well as loss of companionship and consortium.


Medical expenses can quickly add up when a loved one is in critical condition before they pass away. The loss of income and financial support from the deceased can also have devastating consequences for the surviving family members. Finally, loss of companionship and consortium refers to the emotional support, love, guidance, and affection that has been taken away from the surviving family members due to their loved one's untimely passing. While no amount of money can truly compensate for this type of loss, receiving damages for this aspect of wrongful death claims can provide some level of comfort during such a trying time.


Overall, pursuing a wrongful death claim allows families to seek justice for their loved one's passing while also obtaining much-needed financial relief during a challenging period. By understanding the types of damages that can be recovered in these claims, families can better navigate the legal process and ensure they receive fair compensation for their losses.
