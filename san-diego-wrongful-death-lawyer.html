Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifies as wrongful death due to medical malpractice?
Wrongful death due to medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare providers negligence or error leads directly to a patients death. This may include misdiagnosis, surgical errors, medication mistakes, or failure to provide appropriate treatment.
How can I prove that medical malpractice caused my loved ones death?
To prove medical malpractice caused wrongful death, you need to establish that the healthcare provider owed a duty of care, breached that duty through negligence or error, and directly caused the patients death as a result.
Who can file a wrongful death lawsuit in San Diego?
In California, immediate family members such as spouses, domestic partners, children, and sometimes parents or other dependents can file a wrongful death lawsuit if they were financially dependent on the deceased.
What compensation can be sought in a wrongful death case involving medical malpractice?
Compensation may include funeral and burial expenses, loss of income and future earnings, loss of companionship and support, emotional distress suffered by surviving family members, and punitive damages in certain cases.
How long do I have to file a wrongful death lawsuit based on medical malpractice in San Diego?
The statute of limitations for filing a wrongful death lawsuit due to medical malpractice in California is generally two years from the date of the deceased persons passing. Extensions or exceptions may apply under certain circumstances.
