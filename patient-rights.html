Overview of Gresham medical malpractice laws

The right to informed consent is a fundamental principle in healthcare that ensures patients have the necessary information to make decisions about their own medical treatment. This right gives patients the power to understand the risks and benefits of a proposed treatment, as well as any alternative options available.


Informed consent allows patients to actively participate in their healthcare decisions, rather than being passive recipients of care. It empowers individuals to ask questions, seek clarification, and ultimately make choices that align with their values and preferences.


Healthcare providers have an ethical and legal obligation to provide patients with all relevant information regarding their diagnosis, treatment options, potential side effects, and expected outcomes. Informed consent is not just about obtaining a signature on a form; it is about fostering open communication and mutual respect between patients and healthcare professionals.


By upholding the right to informed consent, we uphold the dignity and autonomy of every individual seeking medical care. Patients deserve transparency, respect, and the opportunity to be active participants in decisions that affect their health and well-being. Let us continue to prioritize this essential aspect of patient rights in our healthcare system.

The right to privacy and confidentiality is a fundamental aspect of patient rights. It is essential for patients to feel safe and secure when seeking medical care. Privacy ensures that personal information, medical history, and treatment plans are kept confidential and only shared with authorized individuals.


When patients feel that their information is protected, they are more likely to be open and honest with their healthcare providers. This can lead to better communication, more accurate diagnoses, and effective treatment plans. Patients should have the confidence that their personal information will not be disclosed without their consent.


Confidentiality also plays a crucial role in building trust between patients and healthcare providers. When patients know that their information is being handled with care and respect, they are more likely to seek help when needed and follow through with recommended treatments.


It is the responsibility of healthcare providers to uphold the right to privacy and confidentiality for all patients. This includes maintaining secure electronic medical records, limiting access to sensitive information, and obtaining explicit consent before sharing any personal data.


Overall, the right to privacy and confidentiality is vital for protecting the well-being of patients and ensuring that they receive the best possible care. By respecting these rights, healthcare providers can build strong relationships with their patients based on trust, honesty, and respect.
