The city is the seat of San Diego County, which had a population of nearly 3.3 million people as of 2021. San Diego is known for its mild year-round Mediterranean climate, extensive beaches and parks, its long association with the United States Navy, and its recent emergence as a healthcare and biotechnology development center.

Historically home to the Kumeyaay Native Americans, San Diego has been referred to as the Birthplace of California, as it was the first site visited and settled by Europeans on what is now the West Coast of the United States. Upon landing in San Diego Bay in 1542, Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo claimed the area for Spain, forming the basis for the settlement of Alta California 200 years later. The Presidio and Mission San Diego de Alcalá, founded in 1769, formed the first European settlement in what is now California. In 1821, San Diego became part of the newly declared Mexican Empire, which reformed as the First Mexican Republic two years later. San Diego is the economic center of the San Diego–Tijuana conurbation, the second-most populous transborder metropolitan area in the Western Hemisphere, home to an estimated five million people as of 2022. The primary border crossing between San Diego and Tijuana, the San Ysidro Port of Entry, is the busiest international land border crossing in the world outside of Asia (fourth-busiest overall). Frequently Asked Questions

What is a birth injury lawyer, and how can they help me in San Diego?
A birth injury lawyer specializes in cases where a newborn or mother has suffered injuries due to medical negligence during childbirth. In San Diego, these lawyers can help you by investigating the case, gathering evidence, and representing you in legal proceedings to seek compensation for medical expenses, pain and suffering, and other damages.
How do I know if my babys injury qualifies as medical malpractice?
Medical malpractice occurs when healthcare providers fail to meet the standard of care expected in their profession, resulting in harm. If your babys injury was caused by factors like improper use of delivery tools, failure to monitor fetal distress, or delayed C-section decisions, it may qualify as medical malpractice. Consulting with a specialized lawyer can help determine if you have a viable case.
What should I look for when choosing a birth injury lawyer in San Diego?
When choosing a birth injury lawyer in San Diego, consider their experience with similar cases, track record of successful outcomes, professional reputation, client testimonials, and willingness to offer personalized attention to your case. An initial consultation can also give you insight into their approach and compatibility with your needs.
How long do I have to file a birth injury lawsuit in San Diego?
In California, the statute of limitations for filing a birth injury lawsuit is generally three years from the date of the injury or one year from discovering the injury through reasonable diligence. However, there are exceptions based on specific circumstances. Consulting with a specialized attorney promptly will ensure that you meet all necessary deadlines.
